Curbing Electoral Violence

22nd Feb, 2022

In many developing countries, elections are usually fraught with violence, before during and after the elections. Such violent acts are often perpetrated by the youth for various reasons. Prior to the General Elections in Nigeria, we organize Youth Colloquiums to address this burning issue and proffer realistic and practical measures to serve as alternatives to violence when things go “wrong” before, during and after elections. This includes having early warning systems for impending violence, such as a sudden influx of thugs into a polling area; and having early response systems such as quickly calling the distress lines of security agencies in such situations before things escalate.

Haleemah Oladamade Ahmad

Founder, Acres of Peace

Haleemah Oladamade Ahmad, a social development professional with over 10 years of experience working and volunteering in the development sector, is the CEO and Co-Founder of Acres of Peace, a non-profit dedicated to empowering individuals and communities to live a prosperous life and promoting a just and peaceful society. Passionate about personal and community development, peacebuilding, gender justice and upholding the rights of women as well as integral human development, Haleemah has acquired extensive experience designing, planning, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating peacebuilding and human capacity building programs for projects funded by the US government (US Embassy, Nigeria); the EU delegation in Nigeria; UNFPA, MacArthur Foundation, Tony Blair Institute for Global Change, the Carter Center, amongst others.
Haleemah is also a Senior Research Associate and Facilitator with the Da’wah Institute (DIN), Islamic Education Trust, Nigeria; working at the intersection of building resilience against violent extremism, promoting inter-religious harmony as well as gender justice and human rights. Over the last seven years, she and her team at DIN have researched and analyzed over 200 religious arguments used in recruitment into violent extremist groups, developed alternative and counter-narratives to these arguments, and have trained over 800 Imams, women religious leaders, and youth leaders in using these counter-narratives to strengthen resilience against violent extremism in their communities. This includes the publishing of the book ‘Muslim Relations with Christians, Jews and Others’, in 2018, which responds to 50 faith-based arguments raised by some Muslims to justify aggression against people of other faiths.
Haleemah is a facilitator for the Development Initiative of West Africa (DIWA) where she has led several training sessions for male and female leaders of various organisations on gender justice and women’s rights in Islam; and facilitated workshops for Muslim and Christian leaders to facilitate dialogue and engagement, training of over 300 religious actors in this capacity. She was a UNICEF peer educator on adolescence and reproductive health as a teenager and later served as Team Lead for Al-Ansor Foundation, a non-profit that provides humanitarian aid to indigent individuals. She then co-founded Youths for Youth Development (YFYD), a local nonprofit concerned about capacity building for youths to achieve their maximum potentials.
Haleemah has held leadership positions in several grassroots organizations and has consulted for both local and international development organizations. She has attended several local and international conferences and was a speaker at the Human Rights Defenders Forum (HRDF) organized by the Carter Center in Atlanta, USA, in October 2019, as well as the Insights Forum on Preventing Violent Extremism (PVE) and Promoting Inter-religious Engagement in Nairobi, Kenya in November 2019. She is a Fellow of the Cardinal Onaiyekan Foundation for Peace (COFP) and a recipient of the Rick Love Youing Innovators Award. Haleemah holds a B.Sc in Computer Science from the University of Ilorin, Nigeria; and is currently pursuing a Master of Global Affairs at the University of Notre Dame, USA, specializing in International Peace Studies at the Kroc Institute for International Peace Studies.
